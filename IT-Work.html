Career paths for the future

After my graduation, I would like to become a data analyst working specifically in the financial sector, which includes stock trading, commerce, and banking.  I have always been passionate about both the information technology industry and the financial sector, and such approach will bring me the best of both worlds. Fortunately, I was able to contact with a professional who is currently working in this field, and the conversation I had with him affirmed me that I am choosing the right career path.

Professor Nguyen Hoang Son

 I had contacted Mr. Nguyen Hoang Son, Deputy Director of the Information and Technology Department of the Joint Stock Commercial Bank for Foreign Trade of Vietnam (Vietcombank). In a twenty-minute conversation, we discussed several aspects of the information and technology industry as well as the daily life of a professional working in this field.

Responsibilities

As a director, Mr. Son is responsible for making guidance to his team and controlling the quality outcome of the projects that the bank deploys. Vietcombank has always had good reputation for being one of the leading banks in the country in digital transformation, namely Vietcombank mobile trading app and website. The bank, moreover, had always attempted to bring in the latest technology into their systems. As a result, the projects can be upgrading IT infrastructures, deploying modern services that enhancing customersâ€™ experience.

Relationiships and Networking

Playing the role of a deputy director, Mr. Son had wide relationship networking, in which he works and cooperates with many partners and customers. Vietcombank partners in Information Technology are usually service providers who cooperate with the bank to construct and operate the IT system. Because of the huge number of customers, it is always the priority of the bank to operate the system smoothly, safe, and secure, especially in opening hours when transactions can worth up to billions.

A normal day in the life of an IT professor

During Mr. Sonâ€™s working day, he dedicated the majority of his time to meetings. Meetings are crucial in the IT department and the bank in general because they need to keep track of system continuously. Moreover, the bank is operating at international level, which means that there is absolutely no room for errors even in the closing time of the bank. Any malfunctions need to be resolved at time of discovering to ensure the perfect condition of the system. According to Mr. Son, direct meetings are host in case the decision needs to be approved by several people. On a normal working day, employees in the bank exchange information via phone calls, email, or online meetings if necessary.

 Operations during Covid-19 pandemic

Since the system always need to be in close supervision, I had some questions for Mr. Son regarding way that Vietcombank operated during the social distancing when the Covid-19 pandemic broke out. To not infringe with the rules, the bank policies were to divide employees into different shifts to make sure that there were not too many people at the office. In terms of the system managing, staffs in the IT department would take turn going to the bank to supervise the system, errors detected will be noticed via phone calls or email and online consultation will be hosted to choose the right approach.

Challenging aspects

During his several years of experience, Mr. Son found the most challenging part of his job is timing because of the continually time he spent at work. Working hours are not applied for employees of the IT department, the system determines their working hours. There were many occasions that Mr. Son had to drive all the way from home to the workplace because of the system malfunctions, and many of which are in the middle of the night. In terms of the difficulties at work, he thinks the insufficiency in communicating is the biggest barrier for the work as he stated that there are many times that his staffs did not provide enough information or run into sophisticated interpretation, which prevents people from detecting the errors or working out the right solutions.
